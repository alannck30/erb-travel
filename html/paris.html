Overview

Paris, the enchanting capital of France, is a global center for art, fashion, and gastronomy, famously known as the "City of Light."

Home to over 2 million residents in its core, this romantic city is defined by its stunning Haussmann architecture and iconic landmarks. From the iron curves of the Eiffel Tower to the masterpieces within the Louvre, Paris offers a sensory experience that has inspired dreamers for centuries.

History

Paris's story spans over 2,000 years, from a small Celtic settlement to the world's capital of culture. This city has survived revolutions and renaissances to become the "City of Light."

Roman Lutetia Era

52 BCE

Originally founded by the Parisii tribe, the city was conquered by Romans and named Lutetia. It grew on the Île de la Cité, establishing the heart of what would become one of Europe's most powerful royal capitals.

Medieval Golden Age

1163 CE

This period saw the construction of the Notre-Dame Cathedral and the founding of the Sorbonne. Paris became the intellectual center of Europe, with its winding medieval streets forming the core of the Latin Quarter.

The Great Revolution

1789 CE

The storming of the Bastille marked the beginning of a transformation that toppled the monarchy. This era defined the French identity of "Liberté, Égalité, Fraternité" and turned royal palaces like the Louvre into public museums.

Haussmann's Modern Era

1853 – Present

Under Napoleon III, Baron Haussmann redesigned Paris with wide boulevards, iconic limestone buildings, and grand parks. This created the romantic, uniform look of modern Paris that continues to enchant visitors today.
