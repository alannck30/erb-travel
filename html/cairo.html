Overview

Cairo, the vibrant capital of Egypt, is a sprawling metropolis where ancient history seamlessly blends with modern urban life.

Home to over 20 million people, this magnificent city stands as the gateway to one of the world's oldest civilizations. From the iconic Pyramids of Giza to bustling bazaars and world-class museums, Cairo offers an unforgettable journey through time.

History

Cairo's story spans over 5,000 years, from the ancient pharaohs to the present day. This city has been a witness to some of the most significant events in human history.

Old Kingdom Era (Pharaonic)

2600 BCE

While modern Cairo lies on the east bank, its history begins at Memphis on the west. This was the age of the Great Pyramids of Giza, a time when the region served as the political and cultural center of the world's most advanced early civilization

Fatimid Dynasty Era

969CE

General Jawhar officially founded the walled city of Al-Qahira ("The Victorious"). This period created the "City of a Thousand Minarets," establishing Al-Azhar and the winding streets of Islamic Cairo that still pulse with life today

Saladin’s Reign Era

1176CE

To defend against the Crusaders, Saladin built the massive Cairo Citadel. For the next 700 years, this fortress remained the seat of government, presiding over a golden age of trade, art, and the world-famous Khan el-Khalili bazaar

Modern Era

1860s – Present

Inspired by European urbanism, Khedive Ismail transformed Cairo with grand boulevards and the iconic Tahrir Square. Today, Cairo is a sprawling megacity that blends its ancient soul with a vibrant, modern African and Arab identity

Culture

Experience the vibrant tapestry of Cairo's living culture, where ancient traditions meet modern life. From the aromatic spice markets of the old city to the rhythmic beats of Nile-side music, Cairo is a sensory journey through thousands of years of heritage, reimagined for the 21st century.
